Roundup Cancer Lawsuit Updates

Jury Verdict in Philadelphia Case

In a different Monsanto-related ruling, a jury in Philadelphia found that a lady with NHL had cancer after being exposed to Roundup, and the jury ordered Monsanto to compensate the woman nearly $3.4 million. This is Monsanto's sixth straight legal setback. Monsanto was found guilty of negligence by the jury, which also awarded $462,500 in compensatory damages and $3 million in punitive damages. The defense claimed that the plaintiff's smoking habits were the reason of her NHL.

Missouri Jury Verdict

In a recent Roundup case, a staggering $1.561 billion was awarded in a Missouri state court ruling. James Draeger, Valorie Gunther, and Dan Anderson were each given $61.1 million in damages by a Jefferson City jury, in addition to an astounding $500 million in punitive penalties. They said that the non-Hodgkin's lymphomas they developed were caused by their long-term use of Roundup.

About Roundup Lawsuit

Who Qualifies For The Roundup Lawsuit?

You may have heard about the Roundup lawsuit that has gained national attention in the news and on social media. Roundup is a popular herbicide that is used on lawns around the country, as well as in agricultural settings. There have been recent concerns about a possible link between Roundup use and various types of dangerous and life-threatening cancers. It’s important to understand how to qualify for the Roundup lawsuit if the product has harmed you or a loved one and would like to participate in the class action lawsuit.

Roundup is Linked to Various Cancers

There have been several types of cancers already associated with exposure to Roundup. Non-Hodgkin lymphoma has been in the news as the primary cancer connected to Roundup, but there are other types of cancers associated with the product. Glyphosate is an active ingredient in Roundup, and studies have focused on the correlation between the ingredient and cases of non-Hodgkin lymphoma, including NHL subtypes.

How to Know if You Qualify for the Roundup Lawsuit

There are a few requirements you must meet in order to qualify for the Monsanto lawsuit, such as:

  • You must have been diagnosed with specific cancer types.
  • Your cancer diagnosis must be between 1990 and the present.

You must have been officially diagnosed with one of these qualifying cancers: Non-Hodgkin’s, Mantle Cell, T-cell, Hairy Cell, B-cell, chronic Lymphocytic Leukemia, or another subtype of NHL. Roundup exposure must have contributed to your cancer diagnosis.

If you do not have a valid cancer diagnosis from your doctor but have been experiencing health issues due to Roundup, you may still be able to be included.

You also must not be currently represented by an attorney related to health issues associated with Roundup. You are always welcome to bring a lawsuit against Roundup on your own but must not already have legal representation to partake in the Roundup class action lawsuit.

What is the Focus of the Lawsuit?

Lawsuits have been filed against Monsanto all over the United States. Petitioners claim that Roundup caused them to develop cancer and that Monsanto knew of the dangers and did not warn consumers. Monsanto has been accused of knowing and withholding the potential dangers of using Roundup. It’s also alleged that Monsanto did not maintain quality standards by not properly watching the product once it was released and that the company should have updated the product to make it safer for users.

Who is the Lawsuit for?

The Roundup class action lawsuit is for anyone who has been exposed to Roundup and has been diagnosed with a qualifying cancer. This may include those who regularly use Roundup on their lawns and professionals who use the product in business activities.

What Type of Compensation is Available?

Plaintiffs in the Roundup class action lawsuit could be compensated for their losses for various damages. This may include medical bills, lost wages, pain and suffering, and funeral expenses. Victims may seek compensation for physical injuries, illnesses, and emotional distress from Roundup exposure. Many victims may have been forced to miss work, and they can seek restitution for their lost wages.

Do I Have to Pay for an Attorney?

If you are considering filing a Roundup lawsuit, you may be concerned about how much it will cost to hire an attorney. It’s important to understand that attorneys who handle these types of cases work on a contingency-fee basis. This means you do not have to pay upfront for an attorney to handle your case. Class action attorneys will be paid a percentage of the amount awarded if they win your case and nothing if they are unsuccessful. This fee structure makes it easier for people to afford attorneys to pursue lawsuits against Monsanto.
